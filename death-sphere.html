Death Sphere

Death Sphere is a strategy game. Use your best skills to navigate within a deathly sphere while hunting your twin ghosts. Catch as many as you can in no time, as many others will come to challenge you, but do not touch any surface, or you will be penalised with speed.


This game was built as a capstone project while completing my Master of Human Interface Technology, and it probably represents my first attempt into the world of game design. A UI (main menu) welcomes the player by providing the game’s instructions. At the start of the game, the player is assigned one of four characters to control. Every time the player catches a ghost, the player’s character randomly changes, and the screen turns green (positive feedback). On the other hand, the screen turns red (negative feedback) every time a surface is touched, reducing the player’s speed.

On the top-left corner of the screen, UI components record the time the game started and the number of captured ghosts. A score table is displayed at the bottom-left corner, ranking the time ghosts were captured. Finally, a restart and main menu buttons are shown in the top-right corner. This mini-game was developed using the Unity3D engine, and all the assets were downloaded from the Unity Asset Store.
