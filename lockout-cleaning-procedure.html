Lockout Cleaning Procedure

This e-learning module was developed to help people learn the critical points of a machinery lockout before and after performing the cleaning. This procedure was established in a food manufacturing company. Lockout is the process of turning off machinery and applying a physical lockout device to its lockout point. Types of lockout devices might include padlocks, chains and hasps. Lockout prevents energy from being transmitted or released to machinery, so it cannot be restarted and cause death or severe injury to the person or people servicing the machinery.


This interactive instruction covers the basics of the lockout cleaning procedure to keep everyone safe around machinery. The main sections are classified into three categories:

  1. Key Points, where trainees learn about the “why”, “when”, “how”, and “who” is responsible for locking out machinery.
  2. Lockout, designed to help people learn the steps that authorised personnel must follow to lockout machinery safely.
  3. Unlock, where trainees learn the steps that authorised personnel must follow to unlock machinery safely.

Lastly, the course ends with a quiz to engage the audience and test knowledge retention. This e-learning module was developed using Storyline 360 and Adobe Premiere Pro for video editing.
